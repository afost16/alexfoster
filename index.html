My research in human-robot interaction (HRI) and human-computer interaction (HCI) has helped researchers better conduct research for people living with dementia and their carepartners. I am particularly interested in studying communities and subcultures like these that require extra care in this digital era. Ultimately, I want to develop timeless systems and methods that serve as counterbalances to rapid technological advancement so people can live healthier lives with technology.

Research

I am currently working on a project to detect changes in speech for people living with dementia and mild cognitive impairment using a conversational robot at the R-House Robotics Lab, in collaboration with researchers at DePaul University.
